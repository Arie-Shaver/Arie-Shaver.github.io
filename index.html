Challenges with Usage

One challenge I often encounter when using the TV is the login process for streaming services. Unless I take the time to download the respective app on my phone, I have to manually enter my credentials using the remote. This involves navigating through each letter individually with the arrow keys, which is both tedious and time-consuming. The lack of a more intuitive login method makes this an inconvenient aspect of using a TV for streaming. Additionally, using a phone for gaming or video streaming can sometimes be difficult due to automatic screen rotation. Many mobile devices lack the option to lock orientation within specific apps, causing videos or games to suddenly switch between portrait and landscape modes. This can be disruptive when watching a video in full screen, as an accidental tilt may resize the screen, requiring manual adjustment. In gaming, this can lead to certain controls or UI elements becoming misaligned, making it harder to play comfortably. Finally, on a laptop, many websites and applications are not catered to different screen sizes, often requiring frequent zooming in or out to properly view content. Some sites display text or interface elements that are either too small or too large, making navigation difficult. This inconsistency in design can make using a laptop less efficient, requiring constant manual adjustments to ensure readability.
